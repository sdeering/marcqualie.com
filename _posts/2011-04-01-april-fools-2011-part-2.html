--- layout: post title: "April Fools 2011 (Part 2)" date: 2011-04-01 21:43:00 tags: --- This is second part to my earlier post, which I dedicated to being called the April Fools Google Edition due to the fact Google had so many didn't departments pulling their own pranks. This post is a more broad look on some of the other big companies who took part in amusing the world on sacred April Fools Day.

Mootols Core Localisation
This one actually tricked me, and I ended up making an idiot of myself on Twitter in the process. That's what no caffeine for two days will do to you! Mootools have decided it's a good idea to let programmers write Mootools in their Native language so it's easier to read and use without needing to know English. I thought this was an outrage, and promptly posted my resignation of Mootools, which them made me realise I'm an Idiot. Well played, Mootools. Well Played.

Meebo Carrier Pigeons
Since everyone is so focused on the future these days, Meebo took a strep in the other direction. Why not use an ancient messaging system to send messages to friends? Introducing Meebo Carrier Pigeons! Full details over at the Meebo Blog.

Hootsuite Happy Owls
Well, I was meant to keep this secret according to the private and confidential email I received earlier, but it's just too good to not share. Apparently, they're bored of social integration now and want to try something new and cool, like say, gaming? Enter Happy owls! Looks really familiar, no Idea why though..

There are many others but I've just selected my favourites. If you want a huge list of all the pranks that happened today then head over to Tech Crunch, as they have a huge list of April Fools Pranks 2011.
