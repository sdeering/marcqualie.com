--- layout: post title: "HTC Desire HD Cheaper Than Expected" date: 2010-09-20 12:45:00 tags: --- The Desire HD is starting to roll out across networks in Europe as the launch date nears. Nearly every network is offering it on price plans which beat Blackberry and even the iPhone 4. You can get the phone free on most networks for £35+, which is rather annoying considering I just got a 2 years contract on the HTC Wildfire, oops. Not to worry though, as it will be sold as sim-only from many sites such as Play.com and Amazon (pictured left). The original retail price was going to be £500+ however due to the huge demand and competition between stores, the price keeps dropping, with Amazon now selling for £470. Personally, I believe paying that much for a phone is a bit ridiculous, so I'm still looking forward to the Galaxy S Tab which I am going to write about in my next post. Other options of course include the HTC Desire Z, which is very similar to the G2 which was released in America. It has a full Qwerty keyboard, and looks amazing, however, the only thing putting me off this device is the 800mz processor, which when compared to nearly all other phones these days is a bit behind in terms of performance. On the other hand, it's also quite a bit cheaper then the Desire HD.
